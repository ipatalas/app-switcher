## Configuring Applications

Each application you want to control with AppSwitcher is listed in the **Applications** tab. Here, you can assign specific keys, choose how windows cycle, and decide if an app should launch automatically if it isn't running.

<Screenshot src="/docs/images/settings-applications.png" alt="Applications settings" width="1125" height="729" />

### Adding an Application

Instead of manual path-finding, AppSwitcher helps you pick from what’s already on your system.

<Steps>
1. Click the **+ Add** button in the top-right corner.
2. **Choose your app:** * Select from currently running applications from the list.
    * Use the **Search** bar to find a specific process.
    * Click **Browse for file...** to manually select an `.exe` if it's not running.
3. Once added, press the key you want to use with your modifier.
4. Click **Save & Apply** at the bottom to activate your changes.
</Steps>

### Customizing Behavior (Expansion)

By default, application entries are collapsed to keep the list organized. Click on any entry to expand its advanced settings:

* **When focused (Cycle Mode):** Choose how AppSwitcher behaves when you press the hotkey while the app is already in focus. More on cycle modes in the [next section](../cycle-modes).
* **When not running:** Toggle the **Don't start/Start** switch. If enabled (default), pressing the hotkey while the app is closed will attempt to launch it.

### Removing an Application
Click the **trash icon** next to any hotkey to remove it.

### Editing a Hotkey

Click the **Key Button** on the left side of the application entry (the square containing the letter) and then press the new key you want to assign.
After making your changes, click **Save & Apply** at the bottom to activate them.
